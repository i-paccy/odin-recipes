Fluffy French toast

Fluffy French toast is a delicious, light breakfast made by dipping thick slices of bread (like brioche or challah) into a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt, then cooking them in a buttery skillet until golden brown and fluffy. The result is a soft, airy texture with a lightly crisp exterior, perfect for serving with toppings like maple syrup, powdered sugar, and fresh fruit. It's an indulgent treat that's easy to make and perfect for a cozy morning meal.

Ingredients

  • Flour: This fluffy French toast batter starts with ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • Milk: Milk adds moisture and richness to the batter. You can substitute half-and-half, cream, or your favorite alternative milk if you like.
  • Eggs: Of course, you’ll need eggs for French toast! Three eggs bind the batter together and give it a rich texture.
  • Sugar: A tablespoon of white sugar lends welcome sweetness to this decadent breakfast recipe.
  • Vanilla: A teaspoon of vanilla extract adds complexity and takes the flavor up a notch
  • Cinnamon: Ground cinnamon lends coziness and warmth.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt enhances the flavors of the other ingredients, but it won’t make your French toast taste salty.
  • Bread: You’ll need 12 slices of your favorite thick-sliced bread. Good choices include brioche, sourdough, French bread, or challah.
STEPS
  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon (if using), and salt. Whisk until the mixture is fully combined and slightly frothy.
  2. Place a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a tablespoon of butter and let it melt, swirling it to coat the bottom of the pan.
  3. Dip a slice of bread into the egg mixture, making sure it’s fully soaked but not soggy. Let any excess drip off before placing it in the hot skillet.
  4. Place the soaked bread slice in the skillet and cook for 2-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and fluffy. You can adjust the heat slightly if the bread is browning too quickly.
